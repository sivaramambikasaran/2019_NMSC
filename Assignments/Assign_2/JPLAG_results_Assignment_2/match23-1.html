^^^^^^^^^^^

Consider the polynomial :math:`\
sum_{j=0}^{n} x_j^ml_j(x)`, where
:math:`m\in\{0,1,..n\}` and :math:`l_j(x)`
is the :math:`jth` Lagrange
polynomial. Consider the function :math:`f(x)=x^m`.
For a certain set of
interpolation nodes,say :math:`\{x_0,x_1,..x_n\}` the interpolating
polynomial of the function :math:`f(x)` is

.. math:: p(x) =\
sum_{j=0}^{n} f(x_j)l_j(x)=\
sum_{j=0}^{n} x_j^ml_j(x).

But :math:`f(x)`
is itself a polynomial which also interpolates itself
at the above points.
Since polynomial interpolation is unique, we
conclude that

..
math:: \sum_{j=0}^{n}x_j^ml_j(x)=x^m
