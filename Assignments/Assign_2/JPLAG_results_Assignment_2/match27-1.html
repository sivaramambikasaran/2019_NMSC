Here we will be using of the Theorem: Let :math:`(q_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}`
be the orthogonal family of monic polynomial defined by the Hilbert
space :math:`\mathcal{H}=L^2 ([a,b],p)`. Then there is a sequence
:math:`(a_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}` of real numbers and a sequence
:math:`(b_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}` of positive real numbers such that
\ :math:`\hspace{30pt}q_{n+1}(x)=(x-a_n)q_n(x)-b_n q_{n-1}(x)`,\ :math:`\hspace{5pt} n>0`,
with \ :math:`\hspace{30pt}a_n=\dfrac{(xq_n , q_n)}{\|q_n\|^2}`, and
:math:`b_n=\dfrac{(\|q_n\|^2}{\|q_(n-1)\|^2}` . We will also make use of
the Proposition: Setting :math:`a_0 =1`, the monic Legendre polynomials
are given by
\ :math:`\hspace{30pt}q_n=\sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor{(n)/2}\rfloor} (-1)^k a_k s^{n-2k}`
, with :math:`a_k=\dfrac{n!^2}{k!(n-k)!(n-2k)!}\dfrac{2(n-k)!}{(2n)!}`.
Using the Theorem and the symmetry around :math:`x=0` imply the
existence of a sequence :math:`b_n` of positive numbers such that
\ :math:`\hspace{30pt}q_{n+1}(x)=xq_n(x)-b_n q_{n-1}(x)` Since each
polynomial is monic, it implies that :math:`b_n=a_1(n+1)-a_1(n)`, where
:math:`a_1(n)` denotes the coefficient of :math:`x^{n-2}` in
:math:`q_n`. Using the Proposition, we get
\ :math:`\hspace{30pt}a_1(n)=\dfrac{n(n-1)}{2(2n-1)}\hspace{5pt}` and it
:math:`\hspace{5pt}\Rightarrow \hspace{5pt} a_1(n+1)=\dfrac{n(n+1)}{2(2n+1)}`.
Now, it follows immediately that
\ :math:`\hspace{30pt}b_n = \bigg(\dfrac{n(n+1)}{2(2n+1)} - \dfrac{n(n-1)}{2(2n-1)} \bigg) = \dfrac{n^2}{4n^{2} -1}`
. Hence, we conclude that the Legendre polynomials satisfy the
recurrence
\ :math:`\hspace{30pt}q_{n+1}(x)=xq_n(x)-\bigg(\dfrac{n^2}{4n^{2} -1}\bigg) q_{n-1}(x)`
