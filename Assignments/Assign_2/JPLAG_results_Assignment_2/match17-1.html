- Monic Legendre polynomials on :math:`[-1,1]` are defined as,
:math:`q_0(x)=1` & :math:`q_1(x)=x`.

:
math:`q_n(x)` is a monic polynomial of degree :math:`n` such that
:math:`\int_{-1}^1q_n(x)q_m(x)dx=0 \forall m \ne n`

:
math:`\therefore` The weight function :math:`w(x)=1`.

Now if :math:`u` & :math:`v` are :math:`n` times differentiable on
:math:`[-1,1]` and :math:`v` & its first :math:`(n-1)` derivatives
are zero at both endpoints :math:`x=\pm 1`, then
:math:`\forall n\geq 1`\

 

..
math:: \int_{-1}^1u(x)v^{(n)}(x)dx=(-1)^n\int_{-1}^1u^{(n)}(x)v(x)dx

\

Now if :math:`v_n=\frac{d^n}{dx^n}[(x^2-1)^n]`, we get,

:
math:`v_n=\frac{d^n}{dx^n}`
:math:`[\sum_{k=0}^n \left(\begin{array}{c} n \\ k\end{array}\right)(-1)^kx^{2n-2k}]`\ =\ :math:`[\sum_{k=0}^n \left(\begin{array}{c} n \\ k\end{array}\right)(-1)^k \frac{d^n}{dx^n}x^{2n-2k}]`\ =\ :math:`\frac{(2n)!}{n!}x^n-n\frac{(2n-2)!}{(n-2)!}x^{n-2} \pm .....(1)`\

Hence we see that,

:
math:`q_n=x^n- \frac{n(n-1)}{2(2n-1)}x^{n-2} \pm .....(2)`\

Since :math:`w(x)=1` is odd, we have the three terms recurrence,
given by,

:
math:`q_{n+1}=xq_n- \beta_n q_{n-1}`\

Comparing the coefficient of :math:`x^{n-}` from the above relation &
:math:`(2)`, we obtain

:
math:`\frac{n(n+1)}{2(2n+1)}=-\frac{n(n-1)}{2(2n-1)}- \beta_n`\

This gives that, :math:`\beta_n=\frac{n^2}{4n^2-1}`\

Hence we obtain the recurrence relation as,

:math:`\boxed{
q_{n+1}=xq_n- (\frac{n^2}{4n^2-1})q_{n-1}}`, (proved).

math:`p(x)` is a monic polynomial of degree :math:`n`.

Let :math:`p_n(x)` be the set of all polynomials upto degree
:math:`n`.

We know that :math:`p_n(x)` is a vector space of dimension
:math:`n+1`.

Let {:math:`q_i(x): i=0,1,2,...,n`} be the set of Legendre
polynomials.

Define the inner product for :math:`x \in [-1,1]` in :math:`p_n(x)`
by, :math:`<f,g> = \int_{-1}^1 f(x)g(x) \,dx`; where
:math:`f(x),g(x) \in p_n(x)` .

Then {:math:`q_i(x): i=0,1,2,...,n`} forms an orthogonal set of
vectors :math:`\implies` This set is a basis (As there are exactly
:math:`n+1` elements).

Then, for any :math:`p(x) \in p_n(x)`,
:math:`p(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n \frac{<p(x),q_i(x)>}{<q_i(x),q_i(x)>} q_i(x)`
.

:
math:`\implies \lVert p(x) \rVert_2^2 = \int_{-1}^1 |p(x)|^2 \,dx = \int_{-1}^1 ( \sum_{i=0}^n \frac{<p(x),q_i(x)>}{<q_i(x),q_i(x)>}q_i(x))^2 \,dx`
.

Now since, :math:`\int_{-1}^1 q_i(x)q_j(x) \,dx = 0` for
:math:`i \neq j` .

:
math:`\implies` :math:`\lVert p(x) \rVert_2^2` will be minimized
only if :math:`p(x)=q_n(x)`, because in this case all
:math:`<q_n,q_i> = 0`, for :math:`i \neq n` .

Therefore, :math:`\lVert p(x) \rVert` is minimum :math:`\implies`
:math:`\boxed{
