:math:`T_n(x)=cos(ncos^{-1}(x))` We have to prove that

.. math:: T_{n+1}(x)=2xT_{n}(x)-T_{n-1}(x)

with

.. math:: T_0=1 and T_1=x

We know that

.. math:: cos(n+1)\theta-cos(n-1)\theta=2cos\theta cos n\theta \implies cos(n+1)\theta=2cos\theta cos n\theta +cos(n-1)\theta

\ from this relation we can conclude that,

.. math:: T_{n+1}(x)=2xT_{n}(x)-T_{n-1}(x)

(b)From recurrence relation we have
$T\_{n}(x)=2xT\_{n-1}(x)-T\_{n-2}(x)
:math:`<br><br>=2x`\ (2xT\_{n-2}(x)-T\_{n-3}(x))-T\_{n-2}(x):math:`<br><br>=`\ (4x:sup:`2-1)T\_{n-2}(x)-2xT\_{n-3}(x)\ :math:`<br><br>=`\ (4x`\ 2-1)(2xT\_{n-3}(x)-T\_{n-4}(x))-2xT\_{n-3}(x)\ :math:`<br><br>=`\ (8x:sup:`3-4x)T\_{n-3}(x)-(4x`\ 2-1)T\_{n-4}(x)$......
continuing in this manner at the last stage we will get the coeffitient

:math:`x_k=cos(\frac{2k+1}{2n+2}\frac{\pi}{2}),k=0,1,2,.....n` since the

:math:`\pi`.Therefore,

.. math:: ncos^{-1}(x)=k\pi

.. math:: \implies cos^{-1}(x)=\frac{k\pi}{n}

The range of the inverse cosine is [0, :math:`\pi`], so we get

.. math:: cos^{-1}(x)=0, \frac{\pi}{n},\frac{2\pi}{n},.....\frac{(n-1)\pi}{n},\pi
