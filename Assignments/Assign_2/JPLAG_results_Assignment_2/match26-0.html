Claim:

V_2=\begin{bmatrix}

.. math:: f(x_0)= f(x_1)=f(x_2)=\cdots f(x_n)=0

.. math:: f(x)=C(x-x_0)(x-x_1)(x-x_2)\cdots (x-x_n)

.. math:: \left(\prod_{i=1}^{n}(x_i-x_0)\right)\left(\prod_{i=1}^{n}(x_i-x_1)\right)\left(\prod_{i=1}^{n}(x_i-x_2)\right) \cdots \left(\prod_{i=1}^{n}(x_i-x_{n-1})\right)

.. math:: x_1x_2^2x_3^3x_4^4\cdots x_n^n

:math:`\frac{n(n+1)}{2}`, which is desired degree of the given matrix.

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
p(x)=\sum_{j=0}^n x_j^m \ell_j(x)

.. raw:: latex

f(x) \approx p(x)= \sum_{j=0}^n f_j \ell_j(x)

on comparing with above summation, we get,

.. raw:: latex

f_j=x_j^m \\
f(x)=x^m\\
p(x) \approx f(x)= x^m
\end{align}

since :math:`x^m` is a polynomial for m and hence it is extremely smooth
function, therefore,

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
p(x)=f(x)=x^m\\
p(x)=x^m
\end{align}

Solution 2 (a) To Show that the Legendre polynomials satisfy the
recurrence

:math:`q_{n+1} = xq_n - \left({\dfrac{n^2}{4n^2-1}}\right)q_{n-1}`

Consider L.H.S = :math:`q_{n+1}` ,

| On multiplying it by :math:`q_n` and integrating over -1 to 1, we get
| :math:`\int_{-1}^1 q_n(x) q_{n+1}(x)dx = 0` using orthogonal property.

Now consider R.H.S. =

:math:`xq_n - \left({\dfrac{n^2}{4n^2-1}}\right)q_{n-1}`

Again multiplying it by :math:`q_n(x)` and integrating over -1 to 1 ,we
get

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
&\int_{-1}^1 xq_n^2-\left({\dfrac{n^2}{4n^2-1}}\right)q_{n-1}q_ndx\\
&\int_{-1}^1 xq_n^2dx-\int_{-1}^1\left({\dfrac{n^2}{4n^2-1}}\right)q_{n-1}q_ndx
\end{align}

second term is equal to zero by orthogonality condition, and we know
:math:`\int_{-1}^1 q_n(x) q_m(x)dx = \frac{2}{2n+1}` for all
:math:`m=n`.

Therefore,

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
\int_{-1}^1 xq_n^2dx &= \left(x \frac{2}{2n+1}\right)_{-1}^1- \int_{-1}^1 1.\frac{2}{2n+1} dx\\
&=\left(x \frac{2}{2n+1}\right)_{-1}^1-\left(x \frac{2}{2n+1}\right)_{-1}^1\\
&=0
\end{align}

Thus L.H.S = R.H.S , i.e. legendre polynomial satisfies the given
recurrence.

Solution 3 (a) Consider

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
T_{n+1}(x) &= \cos((n+1) \arccos(x))\\
&=\cos(n\arccos(x)+\arccos(x))\\
&= \cos(n\arccos(x))(\cos(\arccos(x)))-\sin(n\arccos(x))(\sin(\arccos(x)))\\
&= xT_n(x)-\sin(n\arccos(x))\sin(\arccos(x))\\
xT_n(x)-T_{n+1}(x)&=\sin(n\arccos(x))\sin(\arccos(x))
\end{align}

consider

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
T_{n-1}(x) &= \cos((n-1) \arccos(x))\\
&=\cos(n\arccos(x)-\arccos(x))\\
&= \cos(n\arccos(x))(\cos(\arccos(x)))+\sin(n\arccos(x))(\sin(\arccos(x)))\\
&= xT_n(x)+\sin(n\arccos(x))\sin(\arccos(x))\\
T_{n-1}(x)-xT_n(x)&=\sin(n\arccos(x))\sin(\arccos(x))
\end{align}

From above, we get

.. raw:: latex

\begin{align}
xT_n(x)-T_{n+1}(x)&=T_{n-1}(x)-xT_n(x)\\
T_{n+1} &= 2xT_n - T_{n-1}
\end{align}

Clearly, :math:`T_0 = 1` and :math:`T_1 = x`

(b)

We can instead show that :math:`T_{n+1}(x)` is a polynomial of degree
:math:`n+1` with leading coefficient as :math:`2^{n}` for
:math:`n \geq 1`. From (a),

.. math:: T_{n+1} = 2xT_n - T_{n-1}

We'll use induction

For :math:`n=1`

.. math:: T_{2} = 2xT_1 - T_{0}

.. math:: T_{2} = 2x^2 -1

using :math:`T_0 and T_1`. Clearly statement holds.

Now we assume that the statement holds for chebyshev polynomial of
degree :math:`n`.

Consider the recurrence relation

.. math:: T_{n+1} = 2xT_n - T_{n-1}

Here, by assumption :math:`T_n(x)` is a polynomial of degree :math:`n`
with leading coefficient as :math:`2^{n-1}` for :math:`n \geq 1`. and
also :math:`T_{n-1}` is a polynomial of degree :math:`n-1` with leading
coefficient as :math:`2^{n-2}` for :math:`n \geq 1`. Thus, for
:math:`T_{n+1}(x)`, the leading term comes on multiplying :math:`2x`
with :math:`2^{n-1}x^n` which is :math:`2^n x^{n+1}`

Hence, proved.

(d) As we know that :math:`|{\prod_{k=0}^n (x-x_k})|` is monic
polynomial and :math:`x_k` are chebyshev nodes. Also
:math:`T_{n+1}(x)` is chebyshev polynomial which in not monic since

.. math:: {\prod_{k=0}^n (x-x_k})=\frac{T_{n+1}(x)}{2^n}

on taking mod on both sides, we get

.. math:: |{\prod_{k=0}^n (x-x_k})|=|\frac{T_{n+1}(x)}{2^n}|

since

.. math:: \max_{[-1,1]}|{T_{n+1}(x)}| =\max_{[-1,1]}|\cos((n+1) \arccos(x))|\leq 1

therefore,

.. math:: |{\prod_{k=0}^n (x-x_k})|\leq \frac{1}{2^n}
