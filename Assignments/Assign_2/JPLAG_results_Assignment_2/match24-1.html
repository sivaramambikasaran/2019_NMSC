Theorem: Let :math:`(q_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}` be the orthogonal family of
monic polynomial defined by the Hilbert space
:math:`\mathcal{H}=L^2 ([a,b],p)`. Then there is a sequence
:math:`(a_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}` of real numbers and a sequence
:math:`(b_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}` of positive real numbers such that
:math:`\hspace {30pt}q_{n+1}(x)=(x-a_n)q_n(x)-b_n q_{n-1}(x)`,
:math:`n>0`, with :math:`
a_n=\dfrac{(xq_n , q_n)}{\|q_n\|^2}`, and
:math:`b_n=\dfrac{(\|q_n\|^2}{\|q_(n-1)\|^2}` .

Proposition: for :
math:`a_0 =1`, the monic Legendre polynomials are
given by :math:`
q_n=\sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor{(n)/2}\rfloor} (-1)^k`\
:math:`
a_k s^{n-2k}` , with
:math:`a_k=\dfrac{n!^2}{k!(n-k)!(n-2k)!}\dfrac{2(n-k)!}{(2n)!}`\

Using the Theorem, And the symmetry around :math:`x=0` imply the
existence of a sequence :math:`b_n` of positive numbers such that
:math:`
q_{n+1}(x)=xq_n(x)-b_n q_{n-1}(x)`

Since each polynomial is monic,\ :
